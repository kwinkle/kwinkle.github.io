From Ethical Risk to Challenging Norms

Working with high impact applications like healthcare and education makes engagement with robot ethics unavoidable. Proper engagement with the ethical guidance coming out of e.g. the IEEE or EU Comisssion is going to be crucial in achieving real world acceptability and deployment, and one focus of my work is exploring how we can put such guidance into practice as HRI designers/developers.

For example, how do we balance potential ethical risks of anthropomorphism with the fact that anthropomorphic design seems (generally) to enhance and improve social robot 'effectiveness'? How do we design transparent HRI that really aids users in understanding how robots work, whilst maintaining robot credibility and intuitive ease of use?

Additionally however, I work to explore the broader social impact of robots, which I think represents an exciting design opportunity to going beyond 'just' harm reduction and towards exciting applications in which social robots can make a really positive impact on society.

Most recently, that's been in the context of gender-norm breaking robots, as I am particularly interested in the risks and opportunities posed by social robot identity performance - see the video below, or read our recent workhop paper (presented at the Robo-Identity workshop held at HRI 2021) for more.
