Katie Winkle

About Me

Roboticist / (Social) Scientist / Engineer & Designer

Fundamentally, I work to try and minimise social risks/maximise social benefits associated with robot design and deployment. I'm interested in understanding how robots might influence our norms, attitudes and behaviours on the individual interaction level, but also in the ways that robots can reinforce or disrupt existing social relations and inequities. I'm also interested in the ways we *do* robotics, particularly in how we can use participatory and interdisciplinary approaches to minimise risk/maximise benefit.

Before starting at Uppsala I held a Digital Futures Postdoctoral Research Fellowship at KTH Royal Insitute of Technology, working at the Division of Robotics, Perception and Learning. I completed my PhD in Robotics and Autonomous Systems at the Bristol Robotics Laboratory following undergraduate study in Mechnanical Engineering at the University of Bristol.
