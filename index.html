About Me

I completed my PhD on socially assistive robotics as part of the ECHOS group at the Bristol Robotics Laboratory, where I also worked as a Research Assistant on the RoboTIPS project. My thesis was titled "Expert-Informed Design and Automation of Socially Assistive Robots. Now I'm a Digital Futures Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Division of Robotics, Perception and Learning at KTH Royal Institute of Technology, Stockholm. Here at KTH I am leading projects on feminist human robot interaction and designing and automating robots for teens with teens. You can checkout my overarching research interests here.

Latest Updates

Aug 2021 ~ GenR Workshop

I co-organised and hosted the GenR Workshp at ROMAN 2021 - the miro board remains online and publicly available so you can see some of the key discussion points.

March 2021 ~ HRI 2021

I presented two papers at this year's ACM/IEEE International Conference on Human Robot Interaction. This main track paper assesses ethical risk posed by anthropomorphic behaviours in socially assistive robots, and this alt.HRI paper details a study with 300+ Swedish high school students investigating whether we could boost robot credibility whilst also tackling sexist behaviour. See the video for my alt.HRI talk here.

Sept 2020 ~ New Job!

I started my new job as a Digital Futures Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Division of Robotics, Perception and Learning at KTH Royal Institute of Technology.

June 2020 ~ New Publication

Our paper "In-Situ Learning from a Domain Expert for Real World Socially Assistive Robot Deployment" was accepted for this year's RSS conference. The paper details the technical architecture of my couch to 5km robot fitness coach and presents some preliminary evaluation results. My 5 minute video talk will be uploaded and available after the virutal conference event.

Jan 2020 ~ Invited Talk

I travelled to Colorado, USA to give a talk on my work and its ethical implications ("Designing Persuasive Robots, Ethically") at the invitation of Dr Tom Williams; supported by Mines' Computer Science department and the Daniels Fund.
